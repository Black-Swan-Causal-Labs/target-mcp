# Pilot corpus snapshot — 2026-07-08

> **Historical snapshot.** This run predates the **2026-07-19 removal of the
> critical floor**; the `check_critical_floor` step and the "floor FAIL 12/12"
> figures below reflect a construct that no longer exists in the instrument. Kept
> as-is for the record; see `docs/DECISIONS.md`.

First end-to-end corpus run of the instrument. **Illustrative, not a validated
population estimate** (see caveats). Produced by the automated pipeline:
`parse_pmcid` (JATS main text + PMC-hosted supplements) → judge mode
(`claude-sonnet-5`) → `check_critical_floor` → `aggregate_corpus`.

## Sample

12 open-access target-trial-emulation studies retrieved by PMCID from Europe
PMC. Convenience sample, citation-ranked, **COVID-vaccine-heavy** (6 of 12).
Not random. PMCIDs: 8693691, 9989554, 7944975, 8555967, 9020581, 8929389,
11667504, 9547326, 9978748, 10079373, 11043905, 11090892.

## The story these numbers tell

A coherent, TARGET-relevant pattern: **the field has internalized time-zero
discipline but not explicit estimand / identifying-assumption reporting.**
Time zero (6d/7d) is among the best-reported floor content (7d 0.92) — the
immortal-time message has landed. But the causal estimand (6f/7f, 0.33) and
especially the identifying assumptions (6g 0.00, 7g.i 0.17) are systematically
under-reported. Not one of the 12 explicitly stated its target-trial
identifying assumptions per estimand. That is exactly the reporting gap TARGET
was created to close — and it is why every paper fails the (strict) floor.

## Caveats — do not over-read

1. **n = 12, convenience sample, vaccine-heavy.** Rates are illustrative of the
   instrument's behavior, not population estimates for the TTE literature.
2. **Not gold-standard calibrated.** These are the *judge model's* verdicts, not
   validated against human double-coding. "0% report 6g" is the instrument's
   reading, not established fact. Some low rates (e.g. data sources 5 = 0.00)
   likely reflect the rubric's strictness — item 5 demands five axes plus
   quality metrics, so most papers land `partial`, not `reported`. Whether that
   strictness is correct is a calibration question.
3. **Floor is a BSCL overlay**, not TARGET's own tiering (all 12 failing it is a
   property of the overlay, not a claim about TARGET compliance).
4. ~4.4% of evidence quotes still unresolved — residual PDF/table extraction
   noise (design-note steps 4–5 not yet done).

## Implication for build order

The pilot did its job: the pipeline runs end-to-end at scale, supplement
retrieval works, evidence resolves at ~96%. The binding constraint on making
any of these *numbers* a scientific claim is now clearly **gold-standard
validation** (per-item agreement against human coding), not more features.
